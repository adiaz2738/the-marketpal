Helpful Internal Resources

These are helpful resources I've put together myself. These are are based on the research I do and the criteria I look for in stocks when I'm researching companies.

How to Find a Company's Productivity

Once you have your profitability and efficiency, you can then find the core productivity of the business using those two numbers. This one is useful to gauge whether business operations are well-managed

How to Find a Company's Efficiency

This will demonstrate how I find a company's efficiency using the asset turnover ratio. It's important to understand if a business is using their assets efficiently to generate sales, if not, you want to know what's causing the problem and how that will impact your investment.

How to Find a Company's Profitability

This is an important aspect of any business; how much wealth is the company actually generating for its shareholders. When you're going to invest in a company, you ideally would want as much of that revenue going to the bottom line as possible, otherwise, it might be a sign that the company isn't being managed as well as it could be.

How To Calculate The Intrinsic Value Of Stocks Like Warren Buffett

This article explains how to find intrinsic value. I personally use this formula all the time and it's a step up from what I was doing previously.

5 Must-Have Metrics for Value Investors

Just some essential metrics to know as an investor. You'll come across these often when researching companies and you'll likely need to understand them and use them in your analysis of a stock. Key one to focus on first would be bottom line if you don't know what it is; it's basic accounting vocabulary. Then focus on learning the rest of them.

Intrinsic Value Definition

Finding the intrinsic value is one of the first and most important steps when researching a company and it is very important to know both what it is, and how to calculate it.

Market Capitalization Definition

Knowing what the market cap of a company is and how to calculate it is quite handy. I personally use the market cap formula after finding a company's intrinsic value. I will take the intrinsic value I found and multiply by the number of outstanding shares to get the market cap a company would have at the stock price I plug into the formula.

Price-to-Book Ratio (PB Ratio) Definition

Another good metric to see if a stock is over or undervalued. The plus with using this metric is that it's much quicker and easier to calculate.

Price-to-Earnings Ratio - PE Ration Definition

Another very handy metric to understand because you'll come across it very often and it's very useful to understand it's use.
